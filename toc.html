As a Clue Staff, you occupy a unique role that blends theatrical performance, customer service, and game facilitation.


For many Clue Staff, the performance and even customer service elements come naturally after careers full of cultivating these talents. But the game facilitation aspect of hinting requires practice, observation, and restraint.

Hinting demands constant judgment calls.


Every group of players is different. Some feel neglected without assurance and guidance, others prefer to work very independently, even when stumped.

Your goal is to enhance player satisfaction by helping them when they're truly stuck, without robbing them of the joy of discovery.

This module addresses the three critical failure points in hinting:

  • Over-hinting: Giving too much information too soon, robbing players of achievement
  • Poorly hinting: Confusing players further with unclear, distracting, misleading, or narrative-breaking "hints"
  • Under-hinting: Allowing frustration to escalate and diminish the experience

Topics covered in this module:

  • The Hinting Philosophy: Understanding stewing time vs. frustration
  • Reading the Room: Body language and group dynamics
  • Question-Based Hinting Protocol
  • Maintaining Narrative Immersion
  • Timing Your Interventions
  • Game Anomalies and Special Situations
