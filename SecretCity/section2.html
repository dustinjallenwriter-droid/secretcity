Responding to group dynamics and player behaviour


When it comes to hinting, knowing when to hint is often more important than knowing what to say. You won't always be given direct cues as to when it is or isn't the right time to hint. Developing tact often depends on reading unspoken cues from players.


In this lesson, you'll learn how to interpret group dynamics and adapt your approach to keep every team engaged and challenged. By sharpening your observation skills, you'll be able to support players at just the right moments without giving too much away.

The Three Universal Reminders


Secret City's three universal reminders are powerful tools you can use at any time to encourage teamwork and problem-solving. While they're not quite hints, these prompts help groups work more effectively without revealing puzzle solutions, keeping the experience challenging and rewarding.

When to use:
If the entire group is clustered around one puzzle while others remain untouched, or if some players are idle while others work.

How to phrase in character:
'There's so much to explore here—why not divide your forces?' or 'With your numbers, you could investigate several mysteries at once!'

Example:
Six players crowd around a letter puzzle while other challenges are ignored. Suggest splitting up to cover more ground.

When to use:
> If players are making discoveries but not sharing, or if groups are working in isolation and missing out on each other's insights.

How to phrase in character:
'Have you shared your findings with your companions?' or 'Perhaps you and your teammate should compare notes?'

Example:
One player solves a puzzle, but others don't know and start working on it again. Remind them to update each other.

When to use:
If players are rushing, overlooking obvious clues, or making careless errors. Also helpful when important elements are dismissed without examination.

How to phrase in character:
'Let's be thorough in figuring this out,' or 'What did you notice about the postcard... anything else?'

Example:
Players miss a clue hidden in plain sight because they didn't search carefully. Encourage them to slow down and check again.
