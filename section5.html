Adapting Your Approach


Every group that enters an escape room brings its own unique dynamics and needs. Sometimes, this diversity demands flexibility.


As Clue Staff, your ability to adapt your hinting and hosting style for children, families, ESL groups, accessibility requirements, and other special cases is essential.


This lesson will help you recognize anomalies and make sure every player enjoys a positive and immersive experience — no matter the situation.

Learning Objectives


These objectives will guide you through the skills needed to recognize and respond to special situations:

  • Spot unique group needs and situations that require a flexible approach
  • Modify hinting strategies for young players, families, and English language learners
  • Maintain immersion while ensuring all accommodations are met with dignity
  • Tailor your approach for highly experienced or small groups
  • Use core hinting strategies with thoughtful adjustments in special situations

Key Takeaways


Flexibility is key to supporting every group that enters your game. By recognizing anomalies and adapting your approach, you ensure that all players — regardless of age, language, ability, or experience — have a rewarding and memorable adventure.


Remember to apply core hinting principles with empathy and creativity in every special situation.


Embrace adaptability and call on your empathy — your flexibility can make an escape room experience unforgettably inclusive.
